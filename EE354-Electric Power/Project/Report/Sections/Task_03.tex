\section{Task 3: Voltage Sensitivity Analysis}
\label{sec:task3}

\subsection{Method}
Each load bus was independently perturbed by scaling both active and reactive load by
\[
\alpha \in \{-10\%, 0\%, +10\%\},
\]
while keeping all other loads unchanged. For each case, the custom NR solver was executed and the bus-voltage magnitudes were recorded.

For each target load bus, variance and standard deviation of voltage magnitudes were computed:
\begin{align}
\mathrm{Var}(V_b) &= \frac{1}{N}\sum_{m=1}^{N}(V_{b,m}-\bar{V}_b)^2, \\
\sigma(V_b) &= \sqrt{\mathrm{Var}(V_b)}.
\end{align}
An aggregate ranking index was used:
\begin{equation}
\mathrm{RMS\_STD} = \sqrt{\frac{1}{n_b}\sum_{b=1}^{n_b} \sigma(V_b)^2 }.
\end{equation}

\subsection{Discussion}
The sensitivity analysis indicates that perturbations at bus 5 produce the largest voltage spread throughout the network. This result is consistent with the bus location relative to major power-transfer paths and local reactive demand. Buses 6 and 8 show lower system-wide influence for the same $\pm10\%$ perturbation range.
